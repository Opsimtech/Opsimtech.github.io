§ 01 · The Core Problem

The problem with fast models. And slow ones.

Industry has been forced to choose between two bad options. We built OpSimTech because neither is good enough on its own.

Fast · Untrustworthy

Pure ML is fast — but untrustworthy outside training data.

Black-box models fail silently in novel operating conditions. Engineers can't act on predictions they can't explain. The regulator can't accept what the model can't justify.

Trusted · Slow

Full CFD is trustworthy — but it takes hours.

By the time your simulation converges, the operating window has passed, the wind has shifted, the line has moved. Trust without speed is a memory, not a decision.

OpSimTech sits between them. We encode the physics so you don't have to choose.

§ 02 · How it works

The OpSimTech Physics-AI Stack

A three-layer architecture. The ground truth is physics. The acceleration layer is AI. The output is a digital twin engineers and operators can actually use.

L1
Layer 1 · The ground truth

High-Fidelity Physics Core

CFD SPH DEM

Where governing equations are solved at full fidelity. Run offline to build understanding, validate the AI layer, and generate training data. Industrial-grade meshing, validated turbulence models, the lot. This is where physics earns its paycheck.

L2
Layer 2 · The differentiator

Physics-AI Hybrid Layer

PINNs Surrogates Data + Validation

The right AI approach depends on the physics, the available data, and the deployment constraints. We don't preach one method — we engineer the fit.

A

PINNs

Neural solvers trained on physics residuals. The governing equations are encoded directly into the loss — no labeled data needed.

When equations are known & encodable
B

Physics-Guided Surrogates

ROM, GP, or ML models with physics constraints embedded in architecture or loss. Tractable where full PINNs aren't.

When data exists, PINNs are intractable
C

Data-Driven + Physics Validation

High-frequency sensor data dominates the model. CFD/SPH serve as offline validators — not real-time solvers.

When sensor data is abundant

§ 05 · Method Selection

The right Physics-AI method for every problem.

No single algorithm fits every application. The art is matching the method to the physics, the data, and the deployment constraint. Here's how we decide.

/ 01 · Application
Continuous flow fields
(aero, hydro)
PINNs

Governing equations fully encodable; no labeled data needed. The PDE residual is the loss.

/ 02 · Application
Complex manufacturing processes
(electrode mixing, coating)
Physics-Guided Surrogates · ROM / ML

PINNs intractable at 3D + time; physics constraints embedded in architecture or loss instead.

/ 03 · Application
Sensor-rich operational systems
(running fleets, instrumented lines)
Data-Driven + Physics Validation

High-frequency sensor data dominates the runtime model; CFD/SPH serve as offline validators.

/ 04 · All applications
Cross-cutting fidelity strategy
Multifidelity Coupling

Low-fidelity models online; high-fidelity used offline to train, anchor, and validate the twin.

We don't force every problem into a PINN.
We engineer the right solution for your physics.
/ Honesty PINNs remain an active research area. We apply them where they are proven, and use physics-guided ML where they are not. Acknowledging model limits is part of our quality standard.
